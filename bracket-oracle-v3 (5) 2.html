How to Use Bracket Oracle
All data is built in — no fetch required. Open the bracket, analyze games, adjust the formula. That's it.
1
Bracket is ready to go — The 2026 NCAA tournament bracket and all T-Rank efficiency stats are baked in. Just hit ⚡ Analyze All and predictions run instantly.
2
Analyze games — Click ⚡ on any matchup card, or use the bulk buttons per round or region. The full step-by-step breakdown appears in the right sidebar.
3
Override the model — Disagree? Click your pick in the sidebar. Your choice turns purple. The model's original is always preserved so you can compare.
4
Tune the formula — Visit ⚙ Formula to adjust how much each metric matters. Trust analytics? Weight efficiency higher. Trust history? Boost seed weight. Changes take effect on the next Analyze.

How Each Prediction is Made
1Load StatsRead built-in T-Rank stats for both teams. Teams not in the dataset fall back to seed-average estimates, marked with ~.
2Compute EdgesT1 minus T2 for each metric, scaled to comparable units. Positive = T1 advantage, negative = T2 advantage.
3Seed PriorHistorical upset rate for this exact seed matchup over 40+ years of March Madness data.
4Weighted ScoreEach edge × its formula weight (adjustable in ⚙ Formula tab) → summed into one composite score.
5SigmoidConverts raw score to 0–100% win probability via 1/(1+e^−x). Score = 0 means 50/50.
6Monte Carlo1,000 simulated games add gaussian noise to model real game-day variance — teams rarely play exactly to their season average.
7Blend60% model probability + 40% simulation win rate for a more robust final estimate.
8GuardrailsFinal probability clamped to historically valid range — e.g. a #1 seed can't show below ~78%, which has never happened in 40+ years.
